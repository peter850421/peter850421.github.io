Abstract

In interactive image segmentation, the accuracy of the segmentation process can be affected by errors made in previous iterations, as past decisions cannot be altered. To address this challenge, we propose a model that leverages past decisions to enhance current predictions while ensuring order consistency. Our model utilizes a Vision Transformer (ViT) as the backbone and incorporates a segmentation synthesizer and a sequence sensitizer. The segmentation synthesizer generates a segmentation mask for the input image, while the sequence sensitizer generates pixel embeddings that capture the order of clicks.

To enforce order consistency, we employ cosine similarity to compute the pixel embeddings and update the order mask based on the discrepancy between the ground truth and the previous segmentation output. The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance on various interactive segmentation datasets while requiring fewer user clicks. By leveraging past decisions and ensuring order consistency, our model offers an effective and efficient solution for interactive image segmentation.
